## Curriculum Placement

This class continues **Level 4: Ramas — Meta Ads y Storytelling**. It applies
the earlier Flywheel and Hamburguesa/AAIDA doctrine to paid-social investment,
always-on trust content, retargeting, lead follow-up, testimonials, and live
campaign setup readiness.

## Doctrine To Preserve

- Social-media investment is not only budget allocation. It is a sequence that
  buys attention, learning, trust, and better-timed conversion.
- The market may require 12 to 15 successful touches before asking for more
  information. A successful touch requires real user engagement, not only an
  ad impression.
- Flywheel stages require assets:
  - strangers need trust-building content,
  - prospects need value-based follow-up,
  - consumers need testimonial/referral/promoter loops.
- Generic feature claims create commodity competition. The offer needs a unique
  emotional meaning, like legacy, transformation, peace, confidence, or a
  future identity.
- A Meta ad has three practical surfaces:
  - top copy,
  - middle image or video,
  - lower title.
- The middle visual should empathize with the trigger event. The title should
  name the emotional future. The copy should confront the inertia and failed
  alternatives that keep the person stuck.
- Eight always-on "piezas de enamoramiento" should run before the conversion
  ask. The default campaign objective is often video views.
- A conversion campaign can then target people who watched at least 50 percent
  of those videos.
- After the lead arrives, the campaign continues through automated value
  messages. Shorter sales cycles may use eight messages; longer sales cycles
  may require longer weekly sequences.
- Automated messages should continue as value even if the lead replies, but
  stop after purchase.
- Testimonial assets should not feel scripted. They should be elicited through
  emotional, conversational questions that reveal prior doubt, lived
  experience, feeling, application, and advice to someone still undecided.
- Audio quality is part of conversion. The voice should feel close to the
  viewer; use a lavalier microphone or voice/noise enhancement when needed.
- A free recorded/webinar asset or useful GPT can bridge the audience from free
  value into paid programs or referrals.

## Candidate Worksheet

1. Define the buyer's trigger event.
2. Name the emotional future after the offer.
3. List the alternatives already tried and the inertia they create.
4. Create eight non-selling trust videos.
5. Create one conversion ad with variants.
6. Retarget viewers who watched at least 50 percent of the trust videos.
7. Prepare eight value messages for post-lead follow-up.
8. Extend the nurture sequence if the sales cycle is longer than the event
   window.
9. Add at least one natural testimonial.
10. Check audio quality before publishing.

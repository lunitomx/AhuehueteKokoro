## What Went Well

- The command integrates Customer Factory, Funnel Consciente, Oferta Mafia, landing, analytics, tracking, CRM/follow-up, and readback in one executive path.
- The bottleneck map gives a director of marketing or sales a way to decide whether the next investment belongs in traffic, activation, offer, landing, follow-up, or economics.

- CRM/follow-up remains mostly manual unless a future private connector exists; the run correctly treats that as a visibility gate.

## Heutagogical Checkpoint

### What did you learn?

The most expensive acquisition mistake is assuming the bottleneck is traffic. Often the signal is lost after the click: offer, landing, tracking, CRM visibility, follow-up speed, or unit economics.

### What would you change about the process?

Bring unit economics into the readiness table earlier. A channel can look attractive and still be unhealthy if LTV/CPA is unknown or weak.

### Are there improvements for the framework?

Yes. Acquisition runs should always include both maps: Customer Factory for business mechanics and Funnel Consciente for the invited person's decision journey.

### What are you more capable of now?

Kokoro can now receive "necesito mas invitados" and respond as a sales and marketing operator: diagnose the system, locate the bottleneck, protect tracking and private data, and recommend the next investment with confidence.
